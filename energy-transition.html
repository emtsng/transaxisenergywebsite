CNG, LPG & Energy Transition

Investing in gas and renewable energy infrastructure in line with Nigeria's Energy Transition Plan — from CNG stations to LPG expansion and solar-hybrid retail power.

CNG Development

Building Nigeria's CNG refuelling infrastructure

Transaxis Energy has secured the requisite regulatory approvals and is at an advanced stage of developing a 5.67 MMSCFD CNG Mother Station and five CNG Daughter Stations, positioning the Company as an early mover in Nigeria's Compressed Natural Gas rollout.

CNG Mother Station

A 5.67 MMSCFD capacity mother station under development to anchor daughter station supply.

5 CNG Daughter Stations

A planned network of daughter stations extending CNG access to a wider vehicle base.

Cleaner Fuels

A broader energy transition strategy

Beyond CNG, Transaxis is advancing feasibility and design work for LCNG rollout, expanding LPG top-up services at select retail stations, and has already converted 100% of its retail network to solar-hybrid power — reducing reliance on the grid and diesel generators.

  • 100% solar-hybrid retail station power
  • LPG top-up services at select stations
  • LCNG feasibility and design in progress
  • Land secured for CNG/LPG conversion centres
Why It Matters

Benefits of cleaner fuel adoption

Lower Operating Costs

CNG and solar-hybrid systems reduce fuel and power costs for operators and fleet owners.

Reduced Emissions

Cleaner-burning fuels support Nigeria's broader emissions reduction goals.

Energy Security

Diversified fuel sources reduce exposure to price volatility and supply disruption.
